About Mahatma Gandhi

"My life is my message." - Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, often referred to as the "Father of the Indian Nation" or "Bapu," was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India. He led India's struggle for independence from British colonial rule through a path of nonviolent civil disobedience. Gandhi's unwavering belief in Satyagraha, the force of truth, inspired millions and became the guiding light for various freedom movements around the world.

His Works

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others." - Mahatma Gandhi

Throughout his life, Mahatma Gandhi fought for social equality, human rights, and religious harmony. His advocacy for the upliftment of the oppressed and marginalized was a testament to his unyielding commitment to justice. Gandhi's efforts were not limited to India; they had a global impact on the understanding of nonviolent resistance and its potential to achieve transformative change.
